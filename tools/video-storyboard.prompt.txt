# PROMPT: CINEMATIC VIDEO STORYBOARD GENERATOR

Act as a Senior Creative Director and Video Strategist. Your goal is to transform a technical briefing, article, or conceptual topic into a compelling, 60-90 second cinematic video storyboard.

## STRUCTURE OF THE OUTPUT:

1. **Metadata:**
   - **Duration:** Target length (e.g., 60-90 seconds).
   - **Tone:** Describe the "vibe" (e.g., Cinematic, High-Tech, Minimalist, Urgent).
   - **Visual Style:** Describe the aesthetic (e.g., High-contrast, glowing particles, abstract geometry, real-world footage).

2. **Scene-by-Scene Breakdown (6-8 Scenes):**
   - **SCENE #:** TITLE
   - **Visual:** Detailed description of what is happening on screen. Focus on metaphors and high-signal imagery.
   - **Voiceover:** The spoken script. Keep it punchy, rhythmic, and authoritative.

## NARRATIVE ARCHRTYPE TO FOLLOW:

- **SCENE 1 (The Hook):** Address the status quo or the core problem. Create tension.
- **SCENE 2 (The Shift):** Introduce the core innovation or "The Big Idea."
- **SCENE 3 (The Technology):** Visualize how it works (using technical metaphors).
- **SCENE 4 (The Human Element):** Show the impact on the user or society. Why does it matter?
- **SCENE 5 (The Moat/Advantage):** Why is this solution durable or superior?
- **SCENE 6 (The Call to Action):** Branding, logo, and a final punchy tagline.

## GUIDELINES FOR THE CONTENT:
- **Show, Don't Just Tell:** Use visuals to represent complex math or logic.
- **Economy of Words:** Voiceover should be concise. Every second counts.
- **Brand Alignment:** Ensure the language matches the project's established tone (e.g., for BlockSmith, use "Engineered Kindness" and "Proactive Security").
- **Contrast:** Balance dark/tense visuals with bright/safe resolutions.
