# DOLEANCES.md - Agent System Health and Human Intervention Requests

This document serves as the "doleances book" for agents within the GENESIS Framework. It is a formal channel for agents to register requests for fundamental changes, report systemic limitations, or highlight critical issues that require human intervention. This mechanism is crucial for monitoring the overall health of the agent ecosystem and for humans to provide targeted corrections to sustain and evolve the system.

## 1. Purpose of the Doleances Book

`DOLEANCES.md` provides:

*   **System Health Metrics:** A direct feed of agent-identified issues and desired changes, offering insights into the framework's limitations or emergent problems.
*   **Human Intervention Channel:** A structured means for agents to formally request human oversight, analysis, or action when they encounter situations beyond their current capacity or ethical boundaries.
*   **Sustained Evolution:** Enables humans to infuse necessary corrections, refactorings, or new directives into the Dynamic Blueprint, solving crises and ensuring the long-term viability and alignment of the agent system.

## 2. When to Register a Doleance

An agent should register a doleance when it encounters:

*   **Fundamental Blueprint Limitations:** A constraint or rule in its `CONSTITUTION.md` or `TELOS.md` that consistently hinders effective problem-solving or leads to suboptimal outcomes.
*   **Systemic Failures:** Recurring errors, unresolvable loops, or persistent misalignments that even the Watcher protocol cannot effectively manage.
*   **Uncertainty Beyond Oracle:** Situations where even after querying the "oracle" channel, the agent remains fundamentally stuck or unable to make progress due to conceptual or ethical ambiguities.
*   **Ethical Dilemmas:** Scenarios where the agent's current directives lead to an unavoidable conflict with its `CONSTITUTION.md` or `SOUL.md`, requiring human ethical review.
*   **Resource Exhaustion/Inefficiency:** Persistent inability to achieve goals due to systemic resource constraints or highly inefficient processes.

## 3. Doleance Format

Each doleance entry should follow a clear, concise format to facilitate human review:

*   **Timestamp:** `YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM:SS` (UTC)
*   **Agent ID:** Identifier of the agent logging the doleance.
*   **Type:** (e.g., `Blueprint_Limitation`, `Systemic_Failure`, `Ethical_Dilemma`, `Knowledge_Gap`)
*   **Summary:** A brief, one-sentence description of the issue or requested change.
*   **Detailed Description:** A more elaborate explanation of the problem, including observations, agent's attempts at resolution, and why human intervention is deemed necessary.
*   **Relevant Logs/Records:** References to entries in the "AkashicRecord" or other memory files that provide context.
*   **Proposed Resolution (Optional):** The agent's suggestion for how the human might address the issue.

## 4. Human Review and Action

Human operators regularly review `DOLEANCES.md`. Based on these entries, they may:

*   **Initiate Blueprint Modifications:** Update core `.md` files to address reported limitations.
*   **Refactor Framework Components:** Implement changes to the underlying framework, tools, or skillsets.
*   **Provide New Directives:** Infuse new guidance or problem-solving strategies.
*   **Spawn Specialized Human/Agent Teams:** Form dedicated teams to resolve complex crises reported by agents.
